Singular and Plural Nouns

Nouns can be singular or plural. A singular noun names just one person or thing. A plural noun names two or more people or things.


Singular Nouns

Use a singular noun when you are talking about just one thing or person. We often use the word "a" or "an" before a singular noun.

a tent
a taxi
a house
a park
a doctor
a lady
an idea
an oven
an exercise


Articles: A and An

Small words called articles (or determiners) come before nouns to show that a noun is coming next. The two main articles are a and an.

Use an before nouns that start with a vowel sound (a, e, i, o, u).

an insect
an umbrella
an artist
an oven
an armchair
an idea
an eye

Use a before nouns that start with a consonant sound.

a river
a castle


Quick Tip

A few words break this rule because of how they sound, not how they are spelled.

Use a (not an) before uniform, unit, and user, because the u in these words sounds like "you."
Use an (not a) before hour, heir, and honor, because the h in these words is silent.


Plural Nouns

Use a plural noun when you are talking about two or more people, places, or things. Here are the main rules for making nouns plural.


Rule 1: Add s

For most nouns, simply add s to make them plural.

Singular          Plural
a computer        computers
a chair           chairs
a train           trains
a player           players
a teacher         teachers
a taxi            taxis
a mountain        mountains
a river           rivers
an envelope       envelopes
an insect         insects
an oven           ovens
an uncle          uncles


Rule 2: Add es after s, ss, ch, sh, or x

If a noun ends in s, ss, ch, sh, or x, add es to make it plural.

Singular          Plural
bus               buses
glass             glasses
dress             dresses
branch            branches
church            churches
beach             beaches
sandwich          sandwiches
witch             witches
brush             brushes
flash             flashes
box               boxes
fox               foxes


Rule 3: Change y to i and add es

Most nouns that end in a consonant plus y become plural by changing the y to i and adding es.

Singular          Plural
baby              babies
family            families
story             stories
teddy             teddies
fairy             fairies
puppy             puppies
housefly          houseflies
library           libraries
city              cities
lily              lilies
party             parties
dictionary        dictionaries


Rule 4: Just add s after a vowel plus y

If a noun ends in a vowel plus y, simply add s. Do not change the y.

Singular          Plural
key               keys
monkey            monkeys
donkey            donkeys
toy               toys
boy               boys
cowboy            cowboys
day               days
tray              trays
runway            runways
chimney           chimneys
trolley           trolleys
valley            valleys


Rule 5: Change f to v and add es

Many nouns that end in f become plural by changing the f to v and adding es.

Singular          Plural
half              halves
leaf              leaves
shelf             shelves
wolf              wolves
thief             thieves

However, some nouns that end in f are made plural simply by adding s.

Singular          Plural
chief             chiefs
roof              roofs
handkerchief      handkerchiefs
cliff             cliffs
puff              puffs

A few nouns that end in f can be made plural in two ways.

Singular          Plural
scarf             scarfs or scarves
hoof              hoofs or hooves
dwarf             dwarfs or dwarves
wharf             wharfs or wharves


Rule 6: Change fe to v and add s

Most nouns that end in fe become plural by changing the f to v and adding s.

Singular          Plural
knife             knives
wife              wives
life              lives
midwife           midwives


Rule 7: Nouns ending in o

Most nouns that end in o become plural by simply adding s.

Singular          Plural
video             videos
hippo             hippos
zoo               zoos
kangaroo          kangaroos

But some nouns that end in o become plural by adding es.

Singular          Plural
tomato            tomatoes
potato            potatoes
hero              heroes


Rule 8: Nouns with irregular plurals

Some nouns change their spelling completely in the plural form.

Singular          Plural
man               men
woman             women
child             children
person            people
mouse             mice
tooth             teeth
foot              feet
goose             geese


Quick Tip

What is the plural of the kind of mouse you use with a computer? Both mice and mouses are accepted.


Rule 9: Nouns with the same singular and plural form

Some nouns keep the same form whether they are singular or plural.

Singular          Plural
sheep             sheep (not sheeps)
deer              deer (not deers)
fish              fish (not fishes)
aircraft          aircraft (not aircrafts)
salmon            salmon (not salmons)


Quick Tip

When you are talking about different kinds or species of fish, you can use fishes. For example: the various fishes of the Indian Ocean.


Nouns That Are Always Plural

Some nouns are always used in the plural form. They do not have a singular form.

trousers
shorts
jeans
pants
tights
pajamas
glasses
spectacles
goggles
scissors
binoculars
pliers


Nouns That Are Usually Plural

Some nouns are usually used in the plural form.

shoes
sandals
slippers
boots
chopsticks
gloves
clogs
socks


Quick Tip

You can use the phrase a pair of with these plural nouns. For example:

a pair of trousers
a pair of pants
a pair of glasses
a pair of scissors
a pair of chopsticks
a pair of sandals
a pair of gloves
