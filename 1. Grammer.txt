Think of learning English like building a house. Words are the bricks, but
grammar is the mortar that holds them together. Without it, the bricks just
fall apart!

Let's break down exactly what grammar is, why it matters, and the magic
ingredients that make up every sentence you speak or write.


WHAT IS GRAMMAR?

Grammar is simply the set of rules that helps us put words together to make
clear sentences. It is the roadmap that shows us how to speak and write so
that other people can understand us perfectly.

Where does the word come from? The word grammar comes from an ancient Greek
word, grammatike, which means the art of letters or the study of writing.
People have been studying these patterns for thousands of years to make
communication easier!


THE GRAMMAR RHYME

Read this rhyme, remember it well, and you will always know your parts of
speech!

Every person, place, or thing,
Is a noun that we can bring.

Pronouns take a noun's own place,
Like he or she in every case.

Adjectives describe with care,
Showing how things look or wear.

Verbs tell what we do each day,
Run and jump and laugh and play.

Adverbs tell us how or when,
Quickly, softly, now and then.

Prepositions show the place,
Above, below, or next to space.

Conjunctions join both word and thought,
Linking ideas as they're taught.

Interjections shout with cheer,
Wow and Hooray, loud and clear.


TEACHER'S TIP FOR LEARNERS

Don't worry if you don't memorize all eight right now. As you read through
the lessons on speakengl, come back to this poem. You will find that each
time you read it, it makes more and more sense!
