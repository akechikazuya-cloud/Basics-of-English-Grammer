Nouns

A noun is a word that names a person, place, thing, or animal. There are two main types of nouns: common nouns and proper nouns.

Common Nouns

Common nouns are words for people, places, and things. They do not name one specific person or place. They are general names.


Common Nouns for Things

These words name everyday objects.

ruler, pen, crayons, pencil, book, dictionary, courage
chair, table, sofa, loyalty, lamp, carpet, telephone
hammer, bicycle, saw, axe, drill, ladder, lawnmower
spade, ship, truck, ferry, train, bus, laziness
truth, calculator, television, fridge, cooker, computer, printer


Common Nouns for Animals

These words name animals. Many animals have a special name for their young. Here are some examples.

Animal          Its Young

cat             kitten
cow             calf
horse           foal
sheep           lamb
goat            kid
frog            tadpole
fox             cub
elephant        calf
kangaroo        joey
bear            cub
lion            cub
tiger           cub
whale           calf


Common Nouns for Places

These words name different kinds of places.

bank, hotel, library, museum, mall, theater, hospital
airport, gas station, park, farm, zoo, factory, nursery
school, university, office, mosque, temple, shop, gym
post office, police station, restaurant, supermarket
stadium, synagogue, church


Common Nouns for People and Their Jobs

These words name people by the job or role they do.

singer, manager, sailor, gardener
dancer, secretary, pilot, police officer
artist, teacher, driver, plumber
photographer, doctor, writer, farmer
magician, dentist, friend, clerk
athlete, lawyer, brother, technician
